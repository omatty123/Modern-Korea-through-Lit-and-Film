Deep Dive Reference

Kyŏnghŭi

경희 · Na Hyesŏk · 1918

This page isolates two things students usually need fast: a readable network of who pressures Kyŏnghŭi, and a glossary of Korean words whose English renderings shape how we understand the story's feminism, social world, and argument about what it means to be a person.

Part I

Character and Pressure Map

This is not just a family tree. It is a social pressure diagram. The point of the story is that Kyŏnghŭi is surrounded by linked but distinct forces: parents, siblings, marriage connections, household labor, and rumor circulating from outside the home.

Reading tip: the unnamed figures matter precisely because they are often defined by role, not individuality.
Parent

Yi Ch'ŏrwŏn

이철원

Kyŏnghŭi's father. Drives the arranged marriage plot and treats education as acceptable only until it interferes with marriageability.

Immediate family
Parent

Lady Kim

김 부인

Kyŏnghŭi's mother. Proud of her daughter's education and skill, but still caught inside marriage expectations.

Sibling

Older Brother

오라버니

Present mostly through household detail. His existence anchors Kyŏnghŭi inside the family's gender hierarchy.

In-house ally

Older Brother's Wife

오라버니댁

Kyŏnghŭi's sister-in-law. Listens to her, laughs with her, and provides one of the story's warmest domestic spaces.

Sibling pressure

Younger Sister

동생 / 아우

Already married by section 3. Her earlier marriage intensifies the urgency around Kyŏnghŭi's own fate.

Center

Kyŏnghŭi

경희

Educated in Japan, capable in both study and household labor, and increasingly committed to living first as a human being rather than only as a daughter, prospective bride, or obedient woman.

Marriage network

Sŏmunan Lady-in-law

서문안 사돈 마님

Connected through marriage ties. She voices conservative suspicion toward women's education and pushes marriage as common sense.

Marriage pressure

Judge Kim's Family

김 판사 집

The wealthy proposed match. This family matters less as individuals than as the future already chosen for Kyŏnghŭi by others.

Social doctrine

Older Women in the Kin Network

할머니 · 이모님 · 큰어머니

The repeated refrain is the same: education is excessive, marriage is urgent, and a woman's life should stay within inherited limits.

Household labor

Siwŏl

시월이

Maid in the Yi household. Her bond with Kyŏnghŭi reveals a model of everyday reciprocity rather than command alone.

Public rumor

Rice-cake Peddler

떡장수

Essential figure. She carries gossip about girl students into the home and shows how public opinion disciplines women across class lines.

Warning case

Sunam's Mother

수남 어머니

Her grief over her daughter-in-law turns domestic life into an argument about what kind of household Kyŏnghŭi refuses to reproduce.

Family is not the whole story

The page centers Kyŏnghŭi, but the pressure system extends beyond blood ties into marriage alliances and neighborhood talk.

The peddler really matters

The 떡장수 is a mechanism for rumor. She is how the story stages public surveillance of educated women.

Unnamed roles are meaningful

Many women appear as social positions first. That is part of the story's critique, not an accident of thin characterization.

Marriage is structural

Judge Kim's family is less a romance plot than an institutional demand: wealth, status, lineage, and submission bundled together.

Part II

Key Korean Terms and Their English Equivalents

These are the terms that carry the most interpretive weight in the passage and in the assigned English version. The point is not just dictionary meaning, but what each translation choice gains, smooths over, or sharpens.

사람

Person / Human

Core term
Translation
human being

The crucial choice in the translation. It preserves the philosophical force of Kyŏnghŭi's claim that she is a full human subject before being only a woman.

Korean example
먹고만 살다 죽으면 그것은 사람이 아니라 금수이지요.
English rendering
If we live only for food, we are dumb animals, not human beings.
여학생

Girl Student

Social category
Translation
girl student

A strong period choice. More historical than "schoolgirl" and less flattened than "female student."

Korean example
여학생들 손은 비단결 같다는데 네 손은 왜 이러냐.
English rendering
I've heard that girl students have silky-smooth hands, but whatever happened to yours?
사돈 마님

Affinal Lady

Kinship
Translation
lady-in-law

Odd in everyday English, but useful. It signals a marriage-linked social relation rather than a blood relative.

시집 / 시집가다

Marriage into Husband's House

Gendered structure
Translation
marriage / get married

Readable, but thinner than the Korean. The original implies entry into the husband's family system, not only a wedding event.

Korean example
어서 시집을 가거라. 공부는 해서 무엇하니.
English rendering
Hurry up and get married, and be done with this nonsense about education.
시집살이

Life under In-laws

Domestic burden
Translation
living with tough in-laws

Interpretive rather than literal, but effective. It conveys the labor and hardship compressed into the Korean phrase.

Korean example
똑 시집살이한 손 같구나.
English rendering
Your hands feel as if you were living with tough in-laws.
재미

Interest / Delight

Repetition
Translation
interesting / fun / pleased / killing time

The translator varies the English naturally, but the Korean repetition matters. It turns work, curiosity, and self-making into one continuous field.

Korean example
재미도 스럽다.
English rendering
Isn't this interesting!
공부

Study / Learning

Education
Translation
study / education

English separates these more than Korean does. In the story, personal study and social uplift constantly bleed into each other.

금수

Beast / Animal

Contrast term
Translation
animal / beast / dumb animal

The English becomes more varied and polished than the Korean. The original's blunt repetition makes the ethical contrast harsher.

사람 노릇

Doing the Work of Being Human

Ethical claim
Translation
live like a human being

One of the translation's best decisions. It preserves the sense that personhood is active, moral, and lived.

Korean example
그래야 남에게 존대를 받을 뿐 아니라 저도 사람 노릇을 할 것 같아요.
English rendering
Then people will treat me with respect, and I'll be also able to live like a human being.
삼종지도

Three Obediences

Doctrine
Translation
Three Rules of Obedience

A clear and defensible rendering. It keeps the didactic and prescriptive force of the original Confucian phrase.

Korean example
여자의 생명은 삼종지도라는 가정의 교육이다.
English rendering
The Korean family teaches that a woman's life consists in following the "Three Rules of Obedience."
활동사진

Moving Picture

Modern media
Translation
movie

Smooth and modern, but less period-textured than the original phrase for early cinema.

동서남북도 몰라야 복이 많다

Fortune through Ignorance

Free rendering
Translation
Women are better off when they don't know their right hand from left

A free idiomatic move. The literal east-west-south-north disappears, but the misogynistic praise of female ignorance stays intact.
